This project introduces a **Hybrid Minkowski–Log–Cosh (MLC) loss function**
to enhance the robustness of **Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)** models when
learning from **noisy and outlier-contaminated time-series data**.

The proposed loss combines robustness properties (via a Minkowski-style exponent)
with the smoothness and numerical stability of the **Log-Cosh** function,
addressing key limitations of classical loss functions such as
**Mean Squared Error (MSE)** and **Mean Absolute Error (MAE)**.

---

## Loss Function Formulation

Let $y_i$ and $\hat{y}_i$ denote the true and predicted values at time index $i$.
The Hybrid Minkowski–Log–Cosh loss is defined as:

$$
\mathcal{L}(y,\hat{y})=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\left[\log\!\left(\cosh\!\left(y_i-\hat{y}_i\right)\right)\right]^p
$$

where $p>0$ is a tunable exponent controlling the trade-off between
**robustness to outliers** and **error sensitivity**.

**Interpretation**
- $p=1$: recovers the Log-Cosh loss  
- $p\approx2$: exhibits MSE-like behavior  
- $p<2$: improves robustness by downweighting extreme residuals  

---

## Experimental Evaluation

The method was evaluated on **monthly malaria incidence data spanning 2013–2023**.

Key observations:
- Improved training stability under noisy conditions  
- Reduced sensitivity to extreme outliers  
- More reliable long-horizon forecasting compared to MSE and MAE  

The approach maintains **$\mathcal{O}(N)$ computational complexity**, matching
standard regression losses while providing enhanced robustness.
